WEB SITE CODE OF CONDUCT

Effective as of: January 1, 2014
Date of Last Revision: N/A

The HHLEARN Code of Conduct is essential to our learning management system, but your understanding of these rules and requirements is even more important. That's why we have policies for organizations (Terms & Condition) and for individual members. Each user is responsible for understanding this Code of Conduct even if it changes. You are advised to check back often to verify your understanding of the Code.

I. Representation

Every member of the HHLEARN learning system is expected to represent him or herself honestly and respectfully in all situations. Honest and respectful representation includes, but is not limited to, providing only truthful material information on all HHLEARN personal profiles, surveys, forms, and professional licenses data, as applicable. Members are also expected to behave respectfully to all HHLEARN administrators and employees. Members will not consciously misrepresent him or herself to any member of HHLEARN or in their online use of HHLEARN.com.

II. Academic Honesty

Members are expected to maintain the highest standards of academic integrity. Work that is not of the member’s own completion will receive no credit. If a member is uncertain of what these standards are, he or she may consult his or her manager or any HHLEARN administrator or employee for appropriate counsel, but a member's ignorance is no legitimate defense for academic dishonesty. Academic dishonesty includes lying, cheating, sharing coursework of any kind (including on a Social Network), providing your password to another person, using any form of technology to obtain or copy course materials or test questions, and using unauthorized materials on any general, continuing education or competency exam.

  • • The act of lying is to intentionally provide false information or a false statement with the purpose of misleading or with irresponsible regard of the truth. Lying, in both academic and non-academic activities, is impermissible.
  • • Cheating is acting dishonestly in order to gain an unfair advantage. Cheating includes giving or receiving unauthorized aid on any general, continuing education or competency. Cheating also includes using the same material of work and providing it to another member for their use.
  • • Stealing is the act of taking without permission and without intention to return. The prohibition of stealing includes intellectual property, e.g., the forms and publication libraries, course content, reports, etc.
III. Respect for Terms & Conditions

Individual members of HHLEARN are expected to adhere to the Terms & Conditions that were agreed to by your organization (or yourself if signed up as an individual) and are available on every webpage in the bottom footer.

IV. Respect for the Open Exchange of Ideas

Students shall be guaranteed that their First Amendment right of Freedom of Speech will be observed by all District and college members, including other students. Students are encouraged to engage others in thoughtful and meaningful dialogue while refraining from acting or using language with malicious intent.

Classroom Conduct: Students must behave respectfully toward their peers and professors. In the classroom setting, students may not interrupt their classmates or professor, make fun of them or their expressed views, or disrupt the learning environment. It is important to maintain the best learning environment for all students and professors.
