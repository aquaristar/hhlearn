Compliance

The home health industries, homecare nursing, hospice, home medical equipment, and pharmacies, are some of the most regulated industries. We must comply with an every-changing list of regulations, standards, criteria, and laws. HHLEARN is designed to help organizations comply with all industry regulations.

Conditions of Participation

The Centers for Medicare & Medicaid Services (CMS) develops Conditions of Participation (CoPs) and Conditions for Coverage (CfCs) that health care organizations must meet in order to begin and continue participating in the Medicare and Medicaid programs. These minimum health and safety standards are the foundation for improving quality and protecting the health and safety of beneficiaries. CMS also ensures that the standards of accrediting organizations recognized by CMS (through a process called deeming) meet or exceed the Medicare standards set forth in the CoPs/CfCs.

HHLEARN monitors the CoPs and CfCs and incorporates the appropriate content into our courses. By utilizing HHLEARN an organization can provide documented evidence that the applicable content is being taught and retained by employeesIworkofficeaaall just clicks away.

Accreditation

Being accredited is a requirement for being in the home health industry and accreditation standards and criteria are daunting to say the least. Every organization, with the exception of a small few, will have to meet new quality standards in order to be able to bill the Medicare. Mandatory accreditation is CMS's effort to ensure that Medicare beneficiaries have access to high quality services.

Depending on the specific home health industry your organization provides care or supplies products, the number of accrediting bodies with deemed status can vary greatly. HHLEARN maintains a database of all deemed status organizations' standards/criterion and designs our courses around them. HHLEARN even maintains a database that connects courses and standards/criterion and even dynamically modifies a course to exactly match your accrediting body. No one else can do that! Again, reports are available to assist each organization to inform you as to which courses instruct on which standards/criterion.

Right to Know

Knowing what regulations and laws an organization must comply with is difficult. Many organizations operate within the confines of the law, but don't actually know which laws and regulations they are complying with. HHLEARN believes that every employee has a right to know what laws and regulations they are complying with. All applicable laws and regulations for a specific course are listed on the course introduction page along with the course abstract and learning objectives. Click on the image to the right to see the entire page.

In addition to displaying the applicable laws and regulations at the beginning of every course, HHLEARN has reports that can be printed that list the laws and regulations and the specific courses they are taught in. This way you are only a click away from being able to answer questions like, "How do you teach your staff members about bloodborne pathogens?"

Section 508 Compliance

In 1998 the US Congress amended the Rehabilitation Act (29 U.S.C. ยง 794d) to require Federal agencies to make their electronic and information technology accessible to people with disabilities. Section 508 was enacted to eliminate barriers in information technology, to make available new opportunities for people with disabilities, and to encourage development of technologies that will help achieve these goals.

Section 508 requires that all Web site content be equally accessible to people with disabilities. This applies to Web applications, Web pages and all attached files. HHLEARN achieves Section 508 compliance by utilizing all available technologies to ensure that all content is rendered in ways to make universally accessible, e.g., screen reading technology, closed-captioned videos, proper HTML5 coding, etc.

Exclusion Database

The Office of Inspector General (OIG), under this Congressional mandate, established a program to exclude individuals and entities affected by these various legal authorities, contained in sections 1128 and 1156 of the Social Security Act, and maintains a list of all currently excluded parties called the List of Excluded Individuals/Entities (LEIE).

An OIG exclusion has national scope, and is important to many institutional health care providers because the Congress of the United States established a Civil Monetary Penalty ($10,000 for each item or service furnished during the period that the person or entity was excluded) for institutions that knowingly hire excluded parties.

Many organizations don't realize that they have a legal obligation to check potential employees against the LEIE database before they hire them and periodically throughout their employment. HHLEARN has an option that can automate this feature. If your organization elects to utilize this service, your employees will be checked against this database automatically.

State Compliance

There are many laws and regulations that are mandated at the state level. The federal government may dictate minimum standards, but some states choose to make these laws and regulations more state-specific or expand upon them.

HHLEARN has the capability to modify courses at the state level. An example would be our Sexual Harassment training course. If your employees reside in a state with state-specific sexual harassment laws, the employee's course will train and test them on that specific knowledge, all within the confines of the same course.

A benefit of dynamic, state-specific course modification is that if your organization has multiple locations in different states, the course each employee receives and is tested on will contain correct and pertinent information.
